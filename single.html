Contemporary Styles in Sujini

Originally a design used to depict daily life, flowers, trees, flying birds, fish, rider elephants, gods with their various personalities-the power from Durga is reflected in the quality of the tiger’s hair in the ride.

But today the formation reveals details of rural life and Hindu epics, social issues such as genocide against women, electoral violence, education of girls, scenes of domestic violence dominate projects. Health care courses, environmental illustrations, and photographs of women fighting for their rights are very empowering.

The craftsmen used a fine running stitch to make beautiful embroidered motifs of deities, flowers, and peacock designs. In changing times, artisans began embellishing Indian outfits such as sarees, kurtas, dupattas, and other outfits with Sujani embroidery. Home decor items such as cushions and bolts,

Contemporary Home Decor

Bedsheets, and wall hangings are just some of the popular items adorned with Sujani embroidery. Winter clothing such as Stoles and Jackets with Sujani embroidery is made using Tushra Silk fabric. A modern collection of trousers, tunics, dresses, and jackets unique in handmade ghicha and cotton and Tussar silk, with intricate patterns designed by Kalsi and embroidered by Sujani artists.

The Sujani embroidery form, which consists of pieces of old cloth woven together, has its roots in ancient customs. For example, these old pieces of fabric stitched together were considered the most appropriate for newborn babies to be tucked inside, working for a cover purpose, or quilt. Besides, another tradition was supposed to invoke a goddess named "Chitiriya Maa, who was venerated as 'Lady of the Tatters'. From sun and cloud motifs, which symbolized forces of life, to other patterns like that of sacred animals and fertility symbols which were supposed to evoke blessings from Gods.

Contemporary Accessories

It is not just a work of traditional art but is often used to convey social and political messages. The surrounding narrative declares that social change is the basis and purpose of artistic renewal. The tragic news is full of drunkenness, disability, unemployment, or unemployed husbands, cruel mothers-in-law who are ruthless and demanding, debtors. Alternatively, it includes motifs and empowering women-like scenes such as a speaker or public meeting manager, a female judge, or a forum depicting a woman selling goods and making a living.

Today, Sujani embroidery is also made in the southern part of Rajasthan by making patterns for Sarees, dupattas, and other clothing and furniture. Production of this art was done in about 15 villages in India. The rural women of the Muzaffarpur district in Bihar continue to adorn the Sujani style, using combinations of a fine-running stitch and chain stitch.

The Modern Developments of Sujani are some lifestyle accessories and some home décor items are made such as Earings, Folder Bags, Note Pad, Diary cover, etc, based on the specific need of the customer.
